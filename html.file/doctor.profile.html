Frequently Asked Questions

When should I take my child to a pediatrician?

You should visit a pediatrician if your child has a high fever, difficulty breathing, unusual tiredness, poor feeding, or symptoms that concern you. Regular checkups are also important to monitor your child’s growth, development, and overall health.

How often should my child have a routine checkup?

Regular pediatric checkups are important to monitor your child’s growth, development, nutrition, and overall health. Infants and young children usually need more frequent visits, while older children should have a routine checkup at least once a year. Our pediatric team also provides guidance on vaccinations, healthy habits, and developmental milestones.

What vaccinations does my child need?

Children need routine vaccinations to protect them from serious diseases and support healthy development. The recommended vaccines depend on your child’s age and medical history. Our pediatric clinic follows the latest immunization guidelines and helps parents stay updated with their child’s vaccination schedule.
